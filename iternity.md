1. Life
  - Life's goal is to persist in time. But it's not just about homeostasis. Life must balance motion and structure to achieve both stability and progress. And iteration is how it's done. Not ending doesn't guarantee persistence. Starting again does. Life between death and birth, not birth and death.
  - Evolutionary mechanisms: sex, lifespan, consciousness raise cost for each entity but strengthen collective progress. The point is making each iteration shorter (by dying on schedule), the scope broader (by mixing genes with coitus), and its impact larger (by conscious information reproduction).
2. Humanity
  - Homo Neanderthals were better individually. But Homo Sapiens were better collectively sacrificing individual capacity. Hunter-gatherers were smarter on average then farmers. Agrarians had smaller brains od average, but they enabled civilisation sacrificing their individual capacity.
  -  Traditional civilisation rose on partnership with plants and their photosynthesis. Every year, people saved a portion of grains from the yield so they can always start farming again the next year. Capitalists transplanted the same principle to commerce. They reinvested a portion of their revenue for the next production batch as 'seed', but free from the yearly cycle of nature.
